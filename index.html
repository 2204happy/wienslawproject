What is Wien's Law

Wien's Law defines the relationship between the temperature of an object and the peak wavelength of light emmited. The two values (heat and max wavelength) are inversely proportional, meaning that if heat and max wavelength were to be graphed, the graph would be a linear with a negative gradient.

The formula for wiens law is:

λmax = b/T

Which can be rearanged in these ways:

T = b/λmax

b = T·λmax
